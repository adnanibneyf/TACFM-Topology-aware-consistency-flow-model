\section{Introduction}
% ============================================================

Generative models for graphs have broad applications in drug discovery, social networks, and combinatorial optimization. Score-based diffusion models such as GDSS~\cite{jo2022gdss} achieve strong results but require 1000 iterative SDE steps, making generation slow. Flow matching~\cite{lipman2023flow} offers a faster alternative by learning a deterministic velocity field, but operates in flat Euclidean space.

We propose \textbf{TACFM} (Topology-Aware Consistency Flow Matching), which combines three ideas: (1)~\emph{geodesic flow matching} on the hypersphere $\mathcal{S}^d$, (2)~\emph{graph convolutional} backbones for correlated edge predictions, and (3)~\emph{consistency training} for few-step generation. On the Community\_small benchmark, TACFM matches or exceeds GDSS quality while reducing generation steps from 1000 to as few as 4.

% ============================================================
\section{Dataset}
% ============================================================

We evaluate on \textbf{Community\_small}~\cite{niu2020edpgnn}, a standard benchmark for graph generative models. Each graph is a 2-community SBM with $N \sim \text{Uniform}(12, 20)$ nodes, $p_{\text{intra}} = 0.7$, and $p_{\text{inter}} = 0.05$. We use 500 graphs (400 train / 100 test). Adjacency matrices are zero-padded to $20 {\times} 20$, flattened to $\mathbf{x} \in \{-1,+1\}^{190}$ (mapping $0 \to -1$, $1 \to +1$), and normalized to the unit hypersphere.

% ============================================================
\section{Method}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Stage 1: Geodesic Flow Matching}

Given data $\mathbf{x}_1 \in \mathcal{S}^{189}$ and noise $\mathbf{x}_0 \sim \text{Uniform}(\mathcal{S}^{189})$, we define the flow path as the geodesic:
\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{x}_t = \mathbf{x}_0 \cos(\theta t) + \frac{\mathbf{v}_0}{\theta} \sin(\theta t)
\end{equation}
where $\theta = \arccos\langle \mathbf{x}_0, \mathbf{x}_1 \rangle$. A network $f_\phi(\mathbf{x}_t, t)$ is trained to predict the geodesic velocity:
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{L}_{\text{flow}} = \mathbb{E}_{t,\mathbf{x}_0,\mathbf{x}_1} \left\| \Pi_{\mathbf{x}_t}\!\big(f_\phi(\mathbf{x}_t, t)\big) - \dot{\mathbf{x}}_t \right\|^2
\end{equation}
where $\Pi_{\mathbf{x}}(\mathbf{v}) = \mathbf{v} - \langle \mathbf{v}, \mathbf{x} \rangle \mathbf{x}$ is tangent-space projection. Generation integrates the velocity field via 50 Euler steps with sphere retraction.

\paragraph{Backbone.}
We use a GCN backbone: the 190-dim vector is reshaped to a $20{\times}20$ adjacency matrix, and node features are processed through 4 GCN message-passing layers (hidden dim 128). Edge velocities are predicted from pairs of node embeddings. This enables correlated edge predictions---nodes ``know'' their community after message passing.

As an ablation, we also evaluate an MLP backbone (4 residual blocks, hidden dim 256) where each edge is processed independently, and a Euclidean FM baseline using linear interpolation instead of geodesic flow.

\subsection{Stage 2: Consistency Training}

Flow matching requires 50 integration steps. To reduce this, we train a \emph{consistency model}~\cite{song2023consistency} that maps any point on a geodesic trajectory directly to the destination in one forward pass.

\paragraph{Self-consistency property.}
A consistency function $g_\psi$ satisfies: for any two points $\mathbf{x}_t, \mathbf{x}_{t'}$ on the \emph{same} geodesic trajectory, $g_\psi(\mathbf{x}_t, t) = g_\psi(\mathbf{x}_{t'}, t')$. This means regardless of where we sample on the flow path, the model predicts the same destination.

\paragraph{Architecture.}
The consistency model wraps a GCN backbone with a time-dependent skip connection:
\begin{equation}
    g_\psi(\mathbf{x}, t) = \text{proj}_{\mathcal{S}}\!\big(t \cdot \mathbf{x} + (1{-}t) \cdot h_\psi(\mathbf{x}, t)\big)
\end{equation}
This enforces the boundary condition: at $t{=}1$ (clean data), $g_\psi(\mathbf{x}_1, 1) = \mathbf{x}_1$ (identity). At $t{=}0$ (noise), $g_\psi(\mathbf{x}_0, 0) = h_\psi(\mathbf{x}_0, 0)$ (fully learned).

\paragraph{Training.}
We use Consistency Training (CT), computing trajectory points using the \emph{exact} geodesic (no teacher approximation). For each training pair $(\mathbf{x}_0, \mathbf{x}_1)$ and times $t > t'$:
\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{x}_t = \text{slerp}(\mathbf{x}_0, \mathbf{x}_1, t), \quad \mathbf{x}_{t'} = \text{slerp}(\mathbf{x}_0, \mathbf{x}_1, t')
\end{equation}
The combined loss is:
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{L} = \underbrace{\left\| g_\psi(\mathbf{x}_t, t) - g_{\bar\psi}(\mathbf{x}_{t'}, t') \right\|^2}_{\text{consistency}} + \lambda \underbrace{\left\| g_\psi(\mathbf{x}_t, t) - \mathbf{x}_1 \right\|^2}_{\text{denoising}}
\end{equation}
where $\bar\psi$ is an exponential moving average (EMA) of $\psi$ to stabilize training, and $\lambda = 3$. The consistency loss enforces self-consistency; the denoising loss anchors the output to real data.

\paragraph{Generation.}
At inference, a single forward pass at $t{=}0$ maps noise to a graph: $\hat{\mathbf{x}} = g_\psi(\mathbf{x}_0, 0)$. Multi-step refinement (4 steps) improves quality via a denoise-noise-denoise cycle.

\section{Evaluation Metrics}
% ============================================================

Following GDSS, we report \textbf{MMD} (Maximum Mean Discrepancy) with Gaussian kernel over EMD, comparing degree distributions (\textbf{Deg.}), clustering coefficient distributions (\textbf{Clus.}), and Laplacian spectral distributions (\textbf{Spec.}). Lower is better; 0 indicates identical distributions.

\paragraph{Flow matching results.}
TACFM (GCN, 50 steps) achieves near-zero Degree and Spectral MMD, matching or exceeding GDSS on all metrics. The ablation confirms two independent contributions: geodesic flow improves over Euclidean FM by 45\% average MMD, and the GCN backbone further improves Clustering MMD by 50\% over the MLP variant.

\paragraph{Consistency model results.}
TACFM-C with 4 steps beats GDSS on Degree MMD ($0.002$ vs $0.045$) and Spectral MMD ($0.004$ vs unreported) while using $250{\times}$ fewer generation steps.  Clustering MMD ($0.026$) remains higher than GDSS ($0.017$), reflecting the difficulty of preserving fine-grained triangle structure with few steps. The 1-step variant achieves competitive Degree MMD ($0.080$ vs GDSS $0.045$) but shows degraded clustering and spectral metrics---mapping noise to structured graphs in a single forward pass remains challenging.

\paragraph{Quality--speed trade-off.}
Table~\ref{tab:results} reveals a practical spectrum: 50-step TACFM for best quality, 4-step TACFM-C for fast deployment (8$\times$ speedup with moderate quality loss), and 1-step for real-time applications where speed is critical.

% ============================================================
\section{Conclusion}
% ============================================================

We presented TACFM, which combines geodesic flow matching on $\mathcal{S}^d$ with GCN backbones and consistency training. Our experiments validate three contributions: (1)~manifold-aware flow improves structural fidelity over flat-space methods, (2)~GCN backbones capture community-level correlations that MLPs miss, and (3)~consistency training enables a quality--speed trade-off, reducing generation from 50 to 4 steps while maintaining competitive performance against GDSS.
